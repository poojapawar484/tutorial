        • VVSP Details

          In India, the vehicle scrappage policy was officially announced by the government in March 2021. The policy aims to reduce air pollution caused by old and outdated vehicles, boost the auto industry.

          Under the policy, commercial vehicles that are more than 15 years old and personal vehicles that are more than 15-20 years old will be eligible for scrappage. Vehicle owners who choose to scrap their old vehicles and purchase new ones will receive incentives in the form of waivers on registration fees, road tax, and other charges.

          The policy is also expected to boost the recycling industry, as old vehicles will be dismantled and recycled in an environmentally friendly manner. The government has proposed setting up authorized vehicle scrapping facilities across the country to ensure that vehicles are dismantled and recycled in a safe and responsible manner.

        • Recycling and Mining

          As of 2021, the estimated cost of producing one tonne of steel through mining in India is around $400 to $500. As of 2021, the estimated cost of producing one tonne of steel through scrap metal in India is around $250 to $350. Cost of producing steel from scrap metal is typically around 20-30% lower than the cost of producing steel from iron ore. Production of steel through scrap metals will reduce the cost as compared to producing steel through mining. When the price of steel is reduced the final price of vehicle will also reduce.

          Recycling will also reduce the impact of mining on environment.

          By using recycled steel, manufacturers can reduce the cost of manufacturing steel and contribute to a more sustainable future.
